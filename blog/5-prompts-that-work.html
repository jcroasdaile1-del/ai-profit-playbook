I Tested Hundreds of AI Prompts for Building Businesses. Here Are the 5 That Actually Work.

Published June 2025 • 8 min read

Most AI prompts produce generic garbage. I know because I tested hundreds of them over 6 months while trying to build a side business using nothing but ChatGPT and Claude.

The problem isn't the AI — it's that we're asking the wrong questions. Vague prompts get vague answers. But when you add constraints, structure, and context, the same AI becomes genuinely useful.

Here are the 5 prompts that consistently produce actionable, money-relevant output:

1. The Market Finder

Instead of: "Give me business ideas"

I want to find a profitable business idea I can build as a solo founder with AI tools. I have no budget. Analyze: must be digital, buildable in 2 weeks, proven demand, sellable for $10-$100, zero ongoing cost. For each idea give me the product, who buys it, where they hang out, and competitors' weaknesses.

The specificity forces the AI to think critically rather than just list generic "start a dropshipping store" suggestions. You get ideas that are actually scoped to your constraints — budget, timeline, audience, and competitive landscape.

2. The Competitor Deep Dive

Instead of: "Analyze my competitors"

Analyze my top 5 competitors in [your niche]. For each one: their price point, 3 strengths, what their customers complain about (check reviews), their marketing channels, and gaps I could exploit that they're missing.

This prompt surfaces real opportunities. The "customer complaints" angle is especially powerful — it tells you exactly where to differentiate. When you know what people hate about existing products, you know what to build.

3. The Stress Test

Instead of: "Is this a good idea?"

Stress-test this business idea by playing devil's advocate. Give me: top 5 reasons it fails, a mitigation strategy for each, worst case scenario, what needs to be true for it to hit $1K/month, and whether this is a vitamin (nice to have) or painkiller (must have).

Most people skip validation. This prompt forces brutal honesty before you invest time building something nobody wants. The "vitamin vs painkiller" distinction alone has saved me from building things no one would pay for.

4. The Sales Page Writer

Instead of: "Write a sales page"

Write a complete sales page for [product]. Structure: start with their pain (make them feel seen), agitate what happens if unsolved, introduce the solution, list features as benefits, add a social proof section, specify who it's for and who it's NOT for, include a value anchor comparing to alternatives, add a guarantee, write a FAQ that handles the top 5 objections, and end with a PS line.

The output from this prompt is a complete, conversion-optimized sales page — not a vague paragraph. Every section has a specific job in the conversion funnel. This single prompt has saved me days of copywriting work.

5. The Revenue Diagnosis

Instead of: "How do I make more money?"

My business makes $X/month. Traffic comes from Y. Conversion rate is Z%. Of the three levers (traffic, conversion, order value), which is my weakest link? What single change would have the most revenue impact? Give me 3 quick wins for this week and 1 long-term compounder.

This turns ChatGPT into a growth advisor that focuses on your actual bottleneck, not generic advice. By providing real numbers, you get specific, actionable recommendations instead of "try running ads."

The Pattern: Specificity Beats Everything

Notice the pattern? Every effective prompt has:

  • Constraints — budget, timeline, audience limitations
  • Structure — numbered lists, specific sections, exact format
  • Context — real numbers, real situation, real limitations
  • Expected output format — so you get something you can use immediately

The more you constrain the AI, the better the output. Vague in = vague out. Specific in = actionable out.
